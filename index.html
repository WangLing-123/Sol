Aries Rustico Donbel A. Sol

Student

Born and raised in Pembo, Makati City, I had a joyful childhood surrounded by a loving family. I fondly remember playing tag and other outdoor games with children my age. However, my early years were marked by a tragic loss — my biological mother passed away when I was just five years old. Although my memory of her is vague, my family often reminds me of her last wish: for me to finish my education. Holding on to that promise, I carried her words with me as a guiding light.

After graduating from elementary school, I moved from Makati to Sahud Ulan, Tanza, Cavite, to live with my father and continue my studies in high school. As I advanced from Grade 7 to Grade 12, my childhood hobbies began to shift. I found myself more drawn to online games and watching streamers rather than playing outdoors. Despite the increasing difficulty of my subjects, I pressed on and eventually graduated from Amaya School of Home Industries.

I then enrolled at CVSU CCAT to further develop my skills in computer coding. With this new chapter, my interests evolved again — though I still enjoy online games and watching streams, I have also grown fond of reading online manhwa, manga, and light novels. Academically, I struggle to keep up with my classmates and learn at a slower pace. Some people label me as "nonchalant" and don't take my efforts seriously, but I try not to let it discourage me.

College is proving to be a tough and demanding journey, but I am determined to endure and succeed. My goal is not just to graduate, but to gain experience, discover enjoyment in learning, and find deeper meaning in my academic path.

I know that the path I’m on won’t be easy, but I believe the struggles I face now are shaping me into someone stronger and more capable. With every challenge I overcome, I gain not only knowledge but also resilience. I may not be the best in my class, but I will continue to move forward, step by step. In the end, it’s not just about finishing school — it’s about becoming someone my younger self would be proud of, and someone my mother would have been proud to call her child.
